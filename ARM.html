  ARM Team, a sub-team within the UC Santa Cruz club Slugbotics, is a research-based project aimed at developing a low-cost, open-source, modular robotic arm. As a co-lead of the team, I helped guide both the technical and organizational direction. This strengthened my leadership skills and introduced me to team management practices such as maintaining a shared Bill of Materials (BOM) and creating a Notion workspace to streamline communication, task assignments, and documentation among team members.

MECHANICAL

CAD Depiction of the ARM

I helped create create a 3D CAD models using Onshape. The arm was designed to be modular and customizable, allowing for easy iterations. We created components using both Prusa printers and Bamboo Lab X1 Carbons.

KINEMATICS

I implemented forward and inverse kinematics in MATLAB using the Denavit-Hartenberg (DH) convention. This allowed us to compute the exact positions of the end effector based on joint angles and link lengths.


These calculations served as the foundation for simulation, trajectory planning, and closed-loop control. This step was essential for validating workspace and reachability.

PID

PID Ball Following in CoppeliaSim

Using the onboard Raspberry Pi (as its brain), I implemented a ball-following algorithm to detect colored objects in real time. The system processed video frames to extract the position of a target color blob and adjusted movement accordingly using a proportional-integral (PI) controller.

This algorithm was designed as a precursor to object approach and pickup behavior, potentially enabling the robot arm to autonomously locate and interact with specific items.

OPENSAUCE

Our team had the opportunity to demonstrate the ARM project at OpenSauce 2023 in San Francisco, an event that celebrates maker culture, robotics, and open-source innovation.


We showcased the mechanical design, control system demos, and the modular architecture to a large audience. It demsonrated that collaborative open-source development is critical to moving the world towards a better place.
